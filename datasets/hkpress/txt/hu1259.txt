[title]Summer Programme by HKU's Faculty of Science for secondary school students from UK, USA, Canada, Mainland China, Macau &amp; HK (Press Invitation)[/title]
[date]25 Jul 2005[/date]
 
The Faculty of Science of the University of Hong Kong (HKU) is to organise the "Summer Science Institute" from July 26 to 30, 2005 for 158 Form 5 to Form 7 secondary schools students who come from UK, USA, Canada, Mainland China,Macau and Hong Kong. 
The "Summer Science Institute", the first of its kind to be organised by a tertiary institution in Hong Kong, will provide hands-on learning experience for upper-form school students through a variety of integrated science topics and interaction with University teachers and students.
Through the plenary lectures, hands-on science workshops, specialist demonstrations and scientific projects for the students in the Summer Science Institute, students can learn more about everyday science in an interactive and creative way.In addition to enhancing students' science knowledge, the programme also provides students with an early experience of university life through recreational activities and accommodation at HKU's residential hall.
An opening ceremony will be held on July 26, 2005 at which Professor JG Malpas, Pro-Vice-Chancellor of HKU, Mr Lam Chiu Ying, JP, Director of The Hong Kong Observatory, and Dr Frederick Leung, Dean of HKU's Faculty of Science, will be the officiating guests.Details of the opening ceremony are as follows:
