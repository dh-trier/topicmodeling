[title]SHKP and CUHK invite two Nobel Laureates in Economic Sciences to lecture in November[/title]

[date]28 October 2004[/date]


 SHKP and CUHK invite two Nobel Laureates in Economic Sciences
 to lecture in November 
 
Sun Hung Kai Properties (SHKP) and The Chinese University of Hong 
 Kong (CUHK) have invited two Nobel Laureates in Economic Sciences 
 to lecture in Hong Kong from November 9 to 11, in the first of the 
 Sun Hung Kai Properties Nobel Laureates Distinguished Lectures series. 
 A limited number of free tickets will be distributed to the public 
 on a first-come, first-served basis, to give Hong Kong people a chance 
 to benefit from the lecturers' scholarship and contributions to humanity.

The 
 two Nobel Laureates will also share their wisdom and insights with 
 staff of SHKP on 10 November. University and secondary school students 
 are also invited to a special Conversation with Two Nobel Laureates 
 session on 11 November.
The 
 Sun Hung Kai Properties Nobel Laureates Distinguished Lectures are 
 made possible by a generous donation from the title sponsor. The series 
 is intended to promote the dissemination of knowledge and professional 
 and entrepreneurial expertise, and to encourage scholarly pursuits 
 and contributions to humanity in the areas recognized by Nobel Prizes.
Professor 
 A Michael Spence is a Professor Emeritus at Stanford University in 
 the USA and a pioneer in the field of signaling in economics. His 
 model has applications in many fields, in particular corporate governance. 
 Professor Spence's lecture will be on Economic Growth, the Internet 
 and the Evolving Structure of Global Markets: Opportunities and Challenges.
Sir 
 James A Mirrlees is a leading contributor to the theory of information 
 asymmetry in economics. His work provides practical solutions to problems 
 in insurance and credit markets, auctions, organizational structures, 
 employment compensation, tax systems, social insurance, political 
 institutions and other areas. His lecture will be on Ageing and 
 Pensions in the Chinese Economies.
