[title]285 dedicated PolyU staff members honoured for long service[/title]
A total of 285 staff members at The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU) today (18 January) received their Long Service Awards in of their loyal service to the Institution. The ceremony was officiated by PolyU President Professor Timothy W. Tong.
Among this year's award recipients, 88 staff members have served the Institution for 15 years, 93 have served for 20 years, 36 for 25 years, 57 for 30 years and another 11 for as long as 35 years by the close of 2012. They each received a certificate and a souvenir from Professor Tong as a token of appreciation from the University.
Addressing the ceremony, Professor Tong expressed his gratitude to all long-serving staff for their loyal and committed services. He said, "I would like to offer my sincerest gratitude to all awardees. Every post in the University a challenging and demanding one, and we  have set higher goals for you, but you seldom fail our expectation and have helped PolyU to grow from strength to strength." Professor Tong added. 
The Long Service Award scheme was introduced in 1990 to foster a sense of belonging among university staff members. The award presentation ceremony also provides a warm gathering for the long-serving staff and an opportunity for the University to publicly acknowledge their years of dedicated service.
