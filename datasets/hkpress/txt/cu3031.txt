[title]Survey Reveals Encouraging Results of the Employment Situations of 2002 CUHK Graduates[/title]

[date]30 April 2003[/date]


Survey Reveals
 Encouraging Results of the Employment Situations of 2002 CUHK Graduates 
 
 
The employment rate and salary level of 2002 graduates of The Chinese 
 University of Hong Kong are very satisfactory. A graduate employment 
 survey showed as at the end of 2002, around 97% of graduates were either 
 employed or pursued further study and the average monthly salary for 
 those employed was HK$14,735. These figures are particularly encouraging 
 in view of the present economic situation.
The survey was conducted 
 by the Career Planning and Development Centre of the Office of Student 
 Affairs of the University in November and December 2002 by means of 
 questionnaires and telephone interviews. It covered all full-time first 
 degree graduates, and graduates of the degrees of Bachelor of Medicine 
 and Bachelor of Surgery (MBChB) of 2001.
Out of a total of 1,962 
 respondents, 72% of them were employed, 24.7% of them pursued further 
 studies, 2.6% of them were seeking employment.
The average monthly salary 
 for those in employment was HK$14,735. The highest salary earner is 
 a Branch Manager of an insurance company with monthly salary HK$60,000. 
 The average monthly salary for MBChB (Medical) graduates is HK$45,000. 
 The average monthly salary of a number of graduates who are Administrative 
 Officers and Financial Planning Executive is around HK$30,000.
The average number of 
 applications sent out was 47. Students on average attended 9 interviews 
 and obtained 2 offers. And 94.2% of the employed graduates secured their 
 first job offers by the end of September 2002.
Most graduates joined 
 the Commerce and Industry sector (65.5%), followed by Education (17.3%), 
 Social and Public Organisations (10.9%) and Government (6.3%).
The top six career fields 
 for our graduates were: Computer and Information Technology/eBusiness 
 (12%), Teaching (12%), Administration/Management (10.7%), Accounting/Auditing 
 (9.4%), Marketing (7.4%) and Medical and Health Care (6.3%). In terms 
 of job satisfaction, up to 92.8% were satisfied with their current jobs.
