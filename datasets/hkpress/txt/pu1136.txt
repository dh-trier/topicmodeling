[title]PolyU plays host to distinguished seminar on The Intelligent Airport project[/title]
Professor Alison Richard, Vice-Chancellor of the University of Cambridge gave a speech today (24 March) at The Intelligent Airport (TINA) project distinguished seminar jointly organized by The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU)'s Department of Computing and the Friends of Cambridge University in Hong Kong.
PolyU President Professor Timothy W. Tong also welcomed Professor Richard and her colleagues at the opening of this seminar. He also congratulated the Cambridge University on its 800th anniversary, and said the TINA project 'is an excellent example of how applied research serves to make our world a better place to live in'.
Led by the University of Cambridge, TINA is a large applied research project funded by the UK Engineering and Physical Sciences Research Council. The project has strong industrial support and the project team has been collaborating with Arup, BAA, Boeing, Laing O'Rourke, Motorola, Red-M, Tyco and Zinwave. It also involves collaboration and support of Cambridge alumni, and PolyU students and researchers.
The main talk of the seminar was given by Prof. Jon Crowcroft, the Marconi Professor of Communications Systems in the Computer Laboratory of the University of Cambridge and a key member of the TINA project team. Dr Henry Chan of the Department of Computing also presented the work of PolyU students for the TINA project during the seminar.
The seminar was attended by Cambridge alumni in Hong Kong, PolyU students and staff as well as people from industry.
