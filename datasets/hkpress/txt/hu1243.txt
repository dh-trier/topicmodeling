[title]HKU And Chinese CDC Jointly Organize A Symposium On Atypical Pneumonia Medical Experts Discuss Collaborations Of Controlling The Disease (Invitation)[/title]
[date]23 Apr 2003[/date]
 
The Chinese Centre for Disease Control and Prevention (Chinese CDC) and the Faculty of Medicine of the University of Hong Kong (HKU) will organise a Joint Symposium on Atypical Pneumonia on Sunday, April 27, 2003 at HKU's Faculty of Medicine. An Expert Panel Discussion is scheduled for Saturday 26, 2003 before the Symposium.
The objectives of the Symposium and the Expert Panel Discussion are to advocate collaborations between medical professionals and scientists in the Region and to keep them up-to-date of the latest development of the disease. Medical professionals and scientists from Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Taipei, the Chinese University of Hong Kong and the University of Hong Kong including Professor Ruan Li, Director of the Institute for Viral Disease Control and Prevention, Chinese CDC, Professor Hong Tao, Academician of Chinese Academy of Engineering / Professor, Professor Ih-Jen Su, Director of Division of Clinical Research, National health Research Institutes, Taipei, Taiwan, Professor Wen Yu Mei, Professor, Department of Molecular Virology, Shanghai Medical College, Fudan University, Professor Nan-Shan Zhong, Professor, Gunagzhou Medical College, Dr Lo Wing Lok, President of Hong Kong Medical Association, Dr Vivian Wong of Hospital Authority of HKSAR, Professor Leung Ping Chung, Director, Institute of Chinese Medicine of the Chinese University of Hong Kong, Professor Yuen Kowk-Yung, Head of Department of Microbiology, the University of Hong Kong, Professor Peiris, Chief of Virology, Department of Microbiology, the University of Hong Kong and many others are invited to join the expert panel and to deliver talks at the Symposium.
