[title]PolyU plays host to the 9th APacCHRIE Conference[/title]
More than 500 delegates from 37 countries and territories are gathering in Hong Kong to attend the 9th Asia-Pacific Council on Hotel, Restaurant and Institutional Education (APacCHRIE) Conference organized by the School of Hotel and Tourism Management (SHTM) of The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU) from 2 to 5 June 2011.
The Conference was officially opened today (3 June) at PolyU's teaching and research hotel, Hotel ICON by Mr Anthony Lau, Executive Director, Hong Kong Tourism Board; Mr Stanley Yen, Chairman, Landis Management Co. Limited; Ms Cynthia Deale, President of International Council on Hotel, Restaurant and Institutional Education (CHRIE); Professor Manat Chaisawat, President of APacCHRIE; Professor Timothy W. Tong, PolyU President; Dr Andy Nazarechuk, Dean of the William F. Harrah College of Hotel Administration, University of Nevada Las Vegas (Singapore Campus); and Professor Kaye Chon, Dean and Chair Professor of the School of Hotel and Tourism Management, PolyU.
Themed 'Hospitality and Tourism Education: From a Vision to an Icon', the Conference provides delegates as well as the international hospitality and tourism community with an inter-disciplinary platform to exchange information, ideas and views, and to consolidate the research efforts and industry experiences in the field. Together with the APacCHRIE Youth Conference 2011, it will stand as an iconic academic milestone of the industry.
Founded in Hong Kong in 2002 by Professor Kaye Chon, APacCHRIE is the regional advocate of hospitality and tourism education for schools, colleges, and universities offering programmes in hotel and restaurant management, foodservice management and culinary arts. It has played an instrumental role in promoting academic and research exchanges in the Asia-Pacific region over the years.
Some of the key topics featured at the conference include:
Catering, Dining and the Foodservice Industry
Education: Practices and Prospects
Human Resources Issues in Hospitality and Tourism Industry
Information Technology and its Influences
MICE and Event Tourism
Operational and Management Issues of Hotel Industry
Sustainability and Social Responsibility
Service Quality &amp; Customer Relationship Management
Tourism Planning and Destination Marketing
Understanding Consumer Behavior
Other Important Issues of Hospitality &amp; Tourism Industry
