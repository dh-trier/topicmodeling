[title]Curtain falls on 'DesignPlus Fashion Competition 2010'[/title]
Organised by Troels H. Povlsen Care Center under the aegis of The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU)'s Institute of Textile and Clothing, the "DesignPlus Fashion Competition 2010" has come to a fruitful close. A total of 18 participating teams today (30 January) showed their specially designed care apparel for the elderly and the disabled at the prize presentation ceremony and mini-fashion show held on the university campus.
According to the organizer, the aim of this competition is to equip students of our community with the knowledge to show concern for the clothing needs of the elderly and disabled. In the course of this competition, PolyU students have teamed up with other local students to show their talents in fashion design and their concern to the community as well. This year over 100 students took part in the competition. They came from 15 educational institutes, including Diocesan Girls School, Maryknoll Convent School, Jockey Club Ti-I College, Lai King Catholic Secondary School,  Lam Tai Fai College, Sacred Heart Canossian College, Sha Tin Government Secondary School, Shau Kei Wan East Government Secondary School South Island School, STFA Lee Shau Kee College, IVE (Kwai Chung), HKU Space, School of Continuing &amp; Professional Studies-The Chinese University of Hong Kong, PolyU and its affiliated Hong Kong Community College.  The ceremony was officiated by Professor K.Y. Wong, Dean of Faculty of Applied Science and Textiles;  Professor Tao Xiao-ming, Head of Insitute of Textile and Clothing; and Dr. Frency Ng, Director of Troels H. Povlsen Care Apparel Center. Some of the winning entries may be used for making care apparels for the elderly and disabled.
