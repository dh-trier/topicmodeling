The School of Professional Education and Executive Development (SPEED) of The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU) will put on an exhibition to showcase to the public the outstanding works of its design students from 17 to 22 August on campus. Designs on display will include more than 100 pieces of works in art, fashion, interior, product and jewellery design, selected from six design courses including Fundamentals of Art and Design, Fundamentals of Fashion Design, Fundamentals of Interior Design, Fundamentals of Product Design, Fashion Design by Project, and Jewellery Product Design and Development. Each course has a duration of 96 teaching hours.Themed 'after work', the show will be presented in a bar-setting exhibition gallery, facilitating in-depth communication between audiences and designers in a relaxing atmosphere. The exhibition, staged annually by the School, is a unique opportunity for the realization of personal design philosophies for its students. The budding designers this year include Bachelor's or Master's Degree holders.The Exhibition is free and is open to public with details as follows:Date : 17 (Wednesday) to 22 (Monday) August 2005 Time : 10 a.m. to 6 p.m.Venue : sd gallery (Room A101 in Core A, PolyU), School of DesignFurther to the introduction of Government's Continuing Education Fund (CEF), SPEED has been providing various reimbursable design courses under its Credit Accumulation Mechanism (CAM) since 2002, attracting a host of in-service practitioners and interested students of various ages and backgrounds. CAM allows flexibility for learners to study at their own paces and accumulate credits which are valid for lifetime. So far, over 8,000 learners have registered under CAM.Media invitation:Members of the press are welcome to cover the press preview of the exhibition with details as follows: Date : 18 August 2005 (Thursday)Time : 3.30 p.m.Venue : sd Gallery (Room AG101 in Core A, PolyU)
