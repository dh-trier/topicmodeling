[title]PolyU celebrates Inauguration of President Tong[/title]
Nobel Laureates, outstanding alumni and community leaders will join the students and staff of The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU) in a series of events held from 6 to 9 April to celebrate the official installation of Professor Timothy W. Tong, who has assumed office on 1 January 2009, as its new President.  The Presidential Inauguration Ceremony will be held on Wednesday 8 April 2009 at the Jockey Club Auditorium. 
Professor Robert Mundell and Professor John Nash, two Nobel Laureates in Economics, will be among the important guests of the inauguration ceremony.  During their stay in Hong Kong, they will attend a Forum to share their thoughts on the Global Financial Crisis on 7 April.  In addition, Professor John Nash will meet up with the young people at the 'Open Dialogue with Nobel Laureate' session jointly organized by PolyU and the Hong Kong Federation of Youth Groups on 9 April with a view to opening up the eyes and minds of the next generation. 
Four distinguished PolyU alumni will come home on 7 April to speak at a sharing seminar 'Hours of Power'. The speakers include Mr Freeman Lau, Partner of Kan &amp; Lau Design Consultants; Mrs Yvonne Law Shing Mo-han, National Chief Knowledge Officer and Partner of Deloitte Touche Tohmatsu; Professor Ng Sai-ho, Consultant, DLS Management Ltd; and Ir Otto Poon Lok-to, Founder and Manager Director of Analogue Group of Companies.
The 'PolyU Achievements Exhibition' will be launched in the morning of 6 April 2009 to showcase the outstanding research achievements over the past few years. On display will be a new model of myCar jointly developed by PolyU's associate Innovech Company Ltd and the Europe-based design house Giugiaro, a home-grown Electric Vehicle, the award-winning 'Non-Invasive Blood Glucose Meter', recent breakthrough in cancer drugs, etc.  The exhibition is open to the public from 10:00 am to 6:00 pm from 6 to 8 April; and from 10:00 am to 3:00 pm on 9 April (Thursday).

About Professor Robert MundellProfessor Robert Mundell has been Professor of Economics at Columbia University over the past thirty years. As the author of numerous works and articles on economic theory of international economics, he prepared one of the first plans for common currency in Europe and is known as the 'father of the euro'. In 1999, Professor Mundell received the Nobel Prize in Economics for his work on monetary and fiscal policy and currency areas. 
About Professor John NashProfessor John Nash is an American mathematician and economist whose works in game theory, differential geometry, and partial differential equations provide insight into the forces that govern chance and events inside complex systems in daily life. His theories are still used today in market economics, computing, accounting and military aspects. He has served as Senior Research Mathematician at Princeton University in recent years. He shared the 1994 Nobel Prize in Economics with game theorists Reinhard Selten and John Harsanyi.
