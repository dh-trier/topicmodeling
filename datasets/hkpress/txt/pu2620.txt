[title]Visit of Swedish Minister for IT and Energy[/title]
Swedish minister for Information Technology and Energy, Ms Anna-Karin Hatt, visited The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU) yesterday (9 October) and witnessed the signing of Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) between PolyU and Malmö University. 
Joining the delegation were Vice-Chancellor Professor Stefan Bengtsson and Pro Vice-Chancellor Research Professor Hans Lindquist of Malmö University; and Mr Jörgen Halldin, Consul General of Sweden in Hong Kong and Macau. The delegation was warmly greeted by PolyU President Professor Timothy W. Tong, Vice-President (Research) Professor Alexander Wai, and Dean of PolyU School of Design Professor Cees de Bont.
In the presence of Minister Ms Anna-Karin Hatt and Consul General Mr Jörgen Halldin, PolyU President Professor Timothy W. Tong and Professor Bengtsson signed the MoU to strengthen academic exchanges and research collaboration between two institutions.
During the visit, the delegates were briefed on PolyU’s Shenzhen Base and various initiatives to advance design education and social innovation. They also attended a roundtable discussion with PolyU’s design professors after the signing of MoU.
PolyU has established strong ties with Swedish institutions. It has signed nine student exchange agreements with Swedish institutions, including Lund University, Uppsala University, and University of Gothenburg. In the last academic year 2012/13, PolyU received over 20 Swedish exchange students and sent a similar number of PolyU students to its partner institutions in Sweden under the PolyU international exchange programme.
