[title]China-Europa Forum forges common vision for two societies[/title]
About 700 participants from all over China and Europe, including senior government officials, political leaders, renowned academics and influential thinkers, will gather on the campus of The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU) to attend the third Biennial Conference of the China-Europa Forum from 12 to 14 July. It is also the very first time that this large-scale international event is held in Hong Kong.
The three-day Conference is themed on "Converse to Converge: Forging a Common Vision for Chinese and European Societies". It will be opened on 12 July by Mr John Tsang, Financial Secretary of the HKSAR Government; Mr Pierre Calame, President of the China-Europa Forum Foundation; Professor Ji Baocheng, President of the Renmin University of China; and Professor Timothy W. Tong, PolyU President.
The conference is jointly organized by The Hong Kong Polytechnic University, the Renmin University of China, the China-Europa Forum Foundation and the Asian Cultural Forum, and supported by the Charles LÃ©opold Mayer Foundation for Human Progress, the Fok Ying Tong Foundation, Office of the European Union in Hong Kong and Macau, the Home Affairs Bureau and the Central Policy Unit of the HKSAR Government, the Municipal Government of Sichuan Province; and the China Energy Fund Committee.
The Forum aims to bridge the Chinese and European societies by sharing knowledge and experiences; to take common challenges for the development of a legitimate, democratic and efficient global community; to make joint efforts for the global governance founded on bilateral, plurilateral or multilateral dialogues amongst major regional entities, with the view of ensuring a harmonious and peaceful world in solidarity. During the three-day Conference, Chinese and European participants will be grouped under four common challenges, nine themes and 60 groups to discuss on a wide range of issues which are of common interest to both Chinese and European societies.
The nine themes are: (1) City, Territory and Sustainable Development; (2) Social Development and Transformation of Lifestyles; (3) New Models of Economy and Corporate Social Responsibility; (4) China and Europe: Local and Global Governance; (5) Relation between Humanity and Biosphere; (6) Science, Technology and Responsibility toward the Society; (7) Rethinking Education; (8) Law and Justice; and (9) Culture and Arts. And the four common challenges facing Chinese and European societies are: (1) Rebuilding Value Systems; (2) Rethinking the Economic Development Models; (3) Initiating a Participatory and Integrated Governance Model; and (4) Role of China and Europe in the Era of Globalization.
On this auspicious occasion, many prominent guests will also deliver their greetings at the opening. They are Mr Herman Van Rompuy, President of the European Council (who will deliver his speech on video); Mr Pat Cox, former President of the European Parliament; Mr Yang Zigang, Deputy Commissioner of the Ministry of Foreign Affair in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region; Ms Florence Hui, Under Secretary for Home Affairs; Mrs Maria Castillo Fernandez, Head of the Office of the European Union in Hong Kong and Macau; and Mr Benjamin Fok, Board Member of Fok Ying Tung Foundation.
The first day of the Conference will see two keynote speeches by two distinguished speakers, namely Professor Cheng Siwei, former Vice-Chairman of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress who will speak on the topic "Green Economy and Sustainable Development"; and Mr Michel Rocard, former Prime Minster of France, who will speak on "Society to Society Dialogue in the Context of Globalization".  Participants of the conference will then separate into 9 themes groups to discuss on converging themes relating to their selected topics.
The Conference will conclude on 14 July Wednesday afternoon with a speech by Mr Pierre Calame, President of the China-Europa Forum Foundation, on the vision of future China-European relationships; and closing speeches by Dr Gerhard Stahl, Secretary General of the Regional Committee of the European Union; Mr Ye Xiaowen, Party Secretary and Vice President of the Central Institute of Socialism; and Dr Patrick Ho, President of Asia Cultural Forum.
