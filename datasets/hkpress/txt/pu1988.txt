Harvard scholar and University Grants Committee (UGC) Member Professor William Kirby today (1 November) gave a talk on the topic of "The Chinese Century?" on the campus of The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU). The lecture by Professor Kirby has attracted a full house of more than 150 audience comprising staff, students and external guests.
Professor Kirby is warmly welcomed by PolyU President Professor Timothy W. Tong and staff and students of the University. In his welcoming address, Professor Tong said, "Globally, eyes are on Asia and the Chinese mainland and perception has it that the next century belongs to the Chinese mainland. I am sure our audience will be enlightened by Professor Kirby's sharing of his views on the trends in entrepreneurship, education and values of the Chinese mainland."
A historian of modern China, Professor Kirby is the Spangler Family Professor of Business Administration at the Harvard Business School and T. M. Chang Professor of China Studies at Harvard University. He is a Harvard University Distinguished Service Professor. He serves as Director of the Fairbank Center for Chinese Studies and Chairman of the Harvard China Fund. Professor Kirby will be conferred the Honorary Degree of Doctor of Humanities by PolyU at its 16th Congregation to be held on 3 November.
Professor Kirby is no stranger to Hong Kong and he has been serving as a Member of UGC since August 2009. His work examines China's business, economic, and political development in an international context. His current projects include case studies of contemporary Chinese businesses and a comparative study of higher education in China, Europe and the United States. He is Honorary Visiting Professor at Peking University, Nanjing University, Chongqing University, and Fudan University.
