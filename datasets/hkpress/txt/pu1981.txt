[title]PolyU hosts 7th Pan-Pacific Conference on Rehabilitation[/title]
Jointly organized by The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU)'s Department of Rehabilitation Sciences together with The Hong Kong College of Family Physicians, the 7th Pan-Pacific Conference on Rehabilitation cum 2010 Graduate Student Conference on Rehabilitation Sciences was opened today (23 October) by Mr Matthew Cheung Kin-chung, Secretary for Labour and Welfare on the university campus.
This year the Conference is themed on "Mind, Brain and Body". The two-day event has attracted more than 400 participants from Australia, Japan, Pakistan, Singapore, Taiwan, the Philippines, Hong Kong and the Chinese mainland, most of them being scholars, researchers and healthcare professionals.
Since its launch in 1998, the Pan-Pacific Conference on Rehabilitation has become the Region's most well-known academic event in the discipline. The Conference has again provided a platform for clinical personnel, scholars and researchers to exchange views and present their latest research findings.
During the first day, three renowned scholars have delivered their keynote presentations. They are Professor Leeanne Carey from La Trobe University; Professor Victor Sierpina from The University of Texas Medical Branch; and Dr Peter Wayne from Harvard Medical School, Harvard University
On the second day of the conference, Professor Poon Chung-kwong, Professor Dahong Zhuo, Dr. Rainbow Ho, Ms Mabel Chau and Mr Edwin Lau will share their experience of leading a healthy life at the "Health and Wellness" session.
