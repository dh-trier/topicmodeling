[title]Free Tickets of Nobel Laureates' Lectures Awailable for Public[/title]

[date]15 May 2006[/date]


 SHKP and CUHK Present Lectures by Four Nobel Laureates
 Free Tickets Available for Public 
 Sun Hung Kai Properties and The Chinese University of Hong Kong will present the fifth instalment of the widely acclaimed The Sun Hung Kai Properties Nobel Laureates Distinguished Lectures on 22 and 23 May 2006 , where four Nobel laureates will deliver public lectures in different academic fields. The four speakers are world renowned economists and scientists, and it is the first time Nobel laureates in Physics and Chemistry are invited to speak in our lecture series. A limited number of free tickets are reserved for the public. 
Professor Thomas C. Schelling, 2005 Laureate in Economic Sciences, will not be able to come to Hong Kong due to personal reasons. 
Details of the lectures
 Date: 22-23 May 2006 (Monday and Tuesday) 
 Venue: Grand Ballroom, Grand Hyatt Hong Kong , Wanchai 
 Language: English 
 Fee: free of charge 

 22 May 2006 (Monday)
 Session I
  
 9:30-10:45 
  
 Opening Ceremony and Lecture by Professor Robert J. Aumann, 2005 Nobel Laureate in Economic Sciences 
 Topic: War and Peace 
 Session II
  
 11:15-12:15 
  
 Lecture by Professor Robert A. Mundell, 1999 Nobel Laureate in Economic Sciences
 Topic: Chinese Macroeconomic Strategies and the International Monetary System 
 Session III
  
 15:30-16:30 
  
 Lecture by Professor Kurt Wuthrich, 2002 Nobel Laureate in Chemistry
  
 Topic: Structural Biology and Structural Genomics an Interdisciplinary Field for Physicists, Chemists and Biologists 
 23 May 2006 (Tuesday)
 Session IV
 10:00-11:00 
 Lecture by Professor Samuel C.C Ting, 1976 Nobel Laureate in Physics
 Topic: Encounters with Modern Physics
