[title]Encouraging Results of the Employment Situations of 2003 CUHK Graduates[/title]

[date]10 June 2004[/date]


 Survey Reveals 
 Encouraging Results of the Employment Situations of 2003 CUHK Graduates
 Average Salary over $13.8K Highest Salary $85K 
 
The employment rate and salary level of 2003 graduates of The Chinese 
 University of Hong Kong are very satisfactory. A graduate employment 
 survey showed as at the end of 2003, around 97% of graduates were either 
 employed or pursuing further studies and the average monthly salary 
 for those employed was HK$13,831. The highest salary graduate got a 
 monthly salary of $85,000. The average number of offers obtained by 
 an individual graduate is two. These figures are particularly encouraging 
 in view of the unfavorable economic situation due to SARS outbreak in 
 2003. 
The 
 survey was conducted by the Career Planning and Development Centre of 
 the Office of Student Affairs of the University from November to December 
 2003 by means of questionnaires and telephone interviews. It covered 
 all full-time first-degree graduates of 2003. 
Out 
 of a total of 2,502 respondents, around 97% were employed or pursuing 
 further studies. 
The 
 average monthly salary for those in employment was HK$13,831. The highest 
 salary earner is a Medical Practitioner working for a Medical Health 
 Care Centre with monthly salary around HK$85,000. 
Most 
 graduates joined the Commerce and Industry sector (72.5%), followed by 
 Education (13%), Social and Public Organisations (12.2%) and Government 
 (2.3%). 
The 
 top six career fields for our graduates were: Computer and Information 
 Technology / eBusiness (11.1%), Accounting / Auditing (10.6%), Administration 
 / Management (8.5%), Teaching (8.4%), Marketing (7.1%) and Medical and 
 Health Care (6%). In terms of job satisfaction, up to 91.3% were satisfied 
 with their current jobs.
