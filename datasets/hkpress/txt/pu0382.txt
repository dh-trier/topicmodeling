[title]PolyU honours Outstanding Students of the Department of Faculty of Construction and Land Use[/title]
More than 60 excellent students from the Faculty of Construction and Land Use (FCLU) were honoured by The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU) yesterday (1 November) at a presentation ceremony for 2003 Outstanding Students and Dean's Honours List. The ceremony was officiated at by Mr Alexander Tzang, PolyU Deputy President.
This year, four students were named outstanding students of departments of FCLU. They all excelled in both academic and personal development and were nominated by their respective departments as the most outstanding students. The four constituent departments of FCLU are Department of Building and Real Estate, Department of Building Services Engineering, Department of Civil and Structural Engineering and Department of Land Surveying and Geo-Informatics.
Besides, more than 60 students were awarded and placed on the Dean's Honours List. These students are on the top five per cent of their classes, and have an averaged Grade Point Average (GPA) of not lower than 3.5 in 2002/03 academic year.
Speaking at the ceremony, Mr Alexander Tzang said, "It is enlightening to see these outstanding students with flying colours in their academic endeavours. I hope they will continue their hard work and apply what they have learnt to the community."
Awarded students will be presented a certificate and book coupon. The ceremony was graced by the presence of secondary school teachers, principals and parents of the awardees. This year is the second year that the Faculty has organized the award ceremony for Outstanding Students and Dean's Honours List.
