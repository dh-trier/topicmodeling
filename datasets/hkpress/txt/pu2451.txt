[title]PolyU names Digital Factory after Dr Ng Tat-lun[/title]
The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU) has named the Digital Factory on its campus after the late Deputy Council Chairman and Distinguished Alumnus Ir Dr Ng Tat-lun, in recognition of his significant contributions to the development of the higher education sector in Hong Kong, particularly to the University.
Mrs Millen Ng, wife of Dr Ng Tat-lun, Ms Marjorie Yang Mun-tak, Chairman of PolyU Council, and Professor Timothy W. Tong, PolyU President, officiated at the naming ceremony today (5 February).Â  Members of Dr Ng's family, PolyU staff, students and alumni also attended the ceremony.
Addressing the ceremony, Professor Tong said, "Dr Ng had a long affiliation with our Institution over the past fifty years as a student, alumnus and Council member. Not only has he witnessed the growth of our University, but also made significant contribution to its advancement."
Professor Tong also announced the setting up of "Dr Ng Tat Lun Memorial Scholarship" during the ceremony in memory of Dr Ng's tremendous contributions to the development of his alma mater.
Located on CD wing of the PolyU campus, the "Ng Tat Lun Digital Factory" was established in the Department of Industrial and Systems Engineering to provide a technology showcase for teaching and facilities to research in Digital Design and Manufacturing for Hong Kong and the region.
