[title]Logistics student awarded industry scholarship[/title]
The Department of Logistics (LGT) of The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU) recently held its fifth Mediterranean Shipping Company (MSC) Scholarship presentation ceremony on campus.  The event was officiated at by Mr Charles Lee, Marketing Service Manager of MSC.
This year's scholarship winner, Miss Candy Lam Lai-king, a second-year student in logistics, was awarded the scholarship in recognition of her academic excellence and outstanding performance during the screening and interview process. The award includes the tuition fee for her third-year study and a paid summer internship at MSC Hong Kong office.  Candy will also be considered for employment by MSC upon graduation.
During the Ceremony, Dr Daniel Ng, Associate Head of LGT, thanked MSC for their generous support in offering this scholarship for the consecutive fifth year.  Dr Ng said, "The continuous support from MSC will help us realize our mission to meet the education and training needs of Hong Kong's shipping and logistics industry."
Expressing her gratitude at the Ceremony, Candy thanked her teachers at LGT for their ardent support and guidance in equipping her with substantial professional knowledge of the logistics industry.  She looks forward to the excitement of working and enriching her practical experience in MSC, a well-known international liner shipping company.
Initiated by Mr Kevin Schellack, Managing Director of MSC and Member of LGT's Departmental Advisory Committee, the scholarship was established in 2002 with an aim to nurture young professionals for the industry.  Currently MSC is the world's second largest global liner in terms of the capacity employed.
