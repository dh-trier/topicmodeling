[title]PolyU-initiated International Strategic Technology Alliance teams up with Australian Technology Network[/title]
The International Strategic Technology Alliance (ISTA), an institutional network initiated by The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU) to foster applied research and technology transfer among 22 leading tertiary institutes from the Chinese mainland, U.K. and U.S, yesterday (22 July) signed a collaboration agreement with Australian Technology Network (ATN).The Memorandum of Understanding was signed by Dr Lui Sun-wing, Chairman of ISTA and PolyU Vice President (Partnership Development) and Prof. Denise Bradley, Chair of ATN, Vice Chancellor and President of University of South Australia. The signing was witnessed by Mr Alexander Tzang, Honorary Founding Chairman of ISTA and PolyU Acting President and joined by some of the ISTA representatives.ATN is a network of five distinctive and prominent Australian universities including University of South Australia, Queensland University of Technology, University of Technology, Sydney, RMIT University and Curtin University of Technology, which are located in each mainland State. ATN aims to contribute to the social and economic wealth of Australia by building strategic partnerships and undertaking solution based research which is relevant to the expectations of industry and the community.Speaking at the ceremony, Mr Tzang said, 'ISTA and ATN share common goal in promoting the technologies and products of the University members from regional to international level, benefiting both the academia and the industry. By joining the two alliances of 27 universities, I am sure that this agreement will leverage the strengths of both alliances for more opportunities among the member institutes.'Under the agreement, ISTA and ATN will work together to establish a mutually beneficial and cooperative partnership for the promotion of strategic exchange, applied research and development, technology transfer, and institutional improvement in areas of common interest at both alliance and individual member levels. A number of programmes are already in the pipeline between the two alliances.Initiated by PolyU in 1995, ISTA aims to provide an open platform for intellectual exchange and cooperation in the development strategy of technology industry among its member institutes. ISTA also strives to assist its members in commercializing and marketing their research outputs in both domestic and global markets. Current members of the Alliance include Fudan University, Harbin Institute of Technology, Hong Kong Polytechnic University, Huazhong University of Science and Technology, Nanjing Universtiy, Northwestern Polytechnical University, Peking University, Renmin University of China, Shanghai Jiaotong University, Sichuan University, South China University of Technology, Southeast University, Tongji University, Tsinghua University, University of Science and Technology of China, Wuhan University of Technology, Xian Jiaotong University, Zhangshan University, Zhejiang University, Cardiff University, University of Warwick and Purdue University.
